Chapter 2 “Gitting” Started: Code, Version Controls and Collaboration

This week, we’ll jump into a workflow for computational social science. This is less about technical skills, and more about learning good practices for doing research. While these might seem cumbersome or clunky initially, they will help us collaborate with others, fix our own mistakes, and save us time and energy down the road!

2.2 In Class Exercise

Consider the following situation: your team is using the data we developed last week to investigate an outcome of interest. However, time is limited, and you want to maximize efficiency in this collaboration.

  1. What are the potential problems/inefficiencies that could arise with this collaboration? Create your own categorization of all the possible problems.

  2. How could these be addressed? Develop at least three strategies that would limit or eliminate issues.

  3. Choose your preferred solution, and sketch a flowchart for how it would work.
